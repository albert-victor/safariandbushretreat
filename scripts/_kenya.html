About Kenya

Situated on the tropical east coast of Africa, Kenya evokes images of dramatic landscapes, a rich natural heritage, and a cosmopolitan fusion of African, Asian and European cultures. These diverse highlights have made Kenya among the most popular tourist destinations on the continent.

The number one attraction is the wildlife; including the Big 5 and the spectacle of the Great Wildebeest Migration. Spread over an extensive network of national parks and private conservancies, decades of proactive conservation and investment in the hospitality industry reveal a range of destinations and activities that cater to a variety of interests and market segments.
Kenya’s extensive coastline abuts the warm azure waters of the Indian Ocean, making it ideal for those wanting to spend a few days pre- or post-safari soaking up the rays or exploring its fascinating marine life.
Adding the finishing touch to the Kenyan experience is a nation as proud of their culture as they are of their custodianship of its treasures. From the first joyful “Jambo!” (Hello) to the last glimmer of the African sun, Kenya will captivate you!

The Great Migration

Over a million blue wildebeest, 200 000 zebras and a multitude of other antelope proceed on an annual circular route across the Masai Mara National Reserve and Serengeti, in search of grazing. Witness the life and death struggle for survival as the surging herd must cross swollen rivers and outrun the predators that their unmissable presence attracts. This is an African safari experience par excellence.

Big 5 Safari

So named by early hunters as the most dangerous species to encounter, the legendary Big 5 live on in the modern photographic safari. The regal lion, secretive leopard, sentient elephant, moody Cape buffalo and prehistoric rhino are the most sought-after species of the African savanna. Expert professional guides know just where to look, sharing the latest information about these species and the challenges to their future survival.

Beach

Kenya’s beach vacations are a year-round affair, with October to May offering the best weather. With iconic beach destinations such as Diani in the south and Lamu Archipelago in the far north, Kenya’s coast is perfect for an idyllic extension to your safari holiday. Fantastic swimming, diving and fishing.

Mountains

Whether you’d like to take in the views from Mt. Elgon, explore the winding gullies of Hell’s Canyon or pit your technical skills on the slope of Mt. Kenya, the Kenyan highlands are a scenic wonderland and hiker’s paradise; best enjoyed in the Dry Seasons from October to May.

City and Culture

Kenya is culturally and ethnically diverse, as is reflected in the traditional dress, cuisine and fascinating history. Visit a traditional Maasai manyatta, explore Nairobi with a local guide, or ponder its history at a museum. Then, toast your adventure with a ‘Tusker’ and sample the eclectic cuisine.

What is the climate and weather like in Kenya?

Kenya’s climate varies from tropical along the coast to arid in the interior and temperate in the highlands. The country experiences two main rainy seasons: the long rains from April to June and short rains from October to December.

Do I need a visa to travel to Kenya?

Most travelers require a visa for Kenya, which can be obtained online before arrival or at the airport. It’s advisable to check the latest visa requirements for your nationality.

What vaccinations do I need before traveling to Kenya?

Vaccinations for Yellow Fever, Typhoid, Hepatitis A and B, and Diphtheria are recommended, along with malaria prophylaxis. Consult with a healthcare provider for the most current advice.

What currency is used in Kenya, and where can I exchange money?

 
The Kenyan Shilling (KES) is the official currency. Foreign currency can be exchanged at banks, forex bureaus, and certain hotels, with ATMs widely available in urban areas.

What other activities are available in Kenya apart from safaris?

Apart from its world-renowned safaris, Kenya offers a multitude of activities. Enjoy the beautiful beaches of Mombasa, perfect for relaxation and water sports. Nairobi, the country’s vibrant capital, is bustling with restaurants, nightlife, and tourist attractions. It’s also a great base for day trips to nearby sites. For convenient travel within cities, taxis can easily be booked through various apps.

What clothing should I pack for my trip to Kenya?

 
Lightweight and breathable clothing is recommended for daytime, with warmer attire for cooler evenings, especially in the highlands. Safari-goers should pack neutral-colored clothes, comfortable walking shoes, and protection against the sun.

Are there any specific health precautions I should take while traveling in Kenya?

 
Ensure to drink bottled or boiled water, protect yourself from mosquito bites to prevent malaria, and practice good hygiene. Consider getting travel insurance that covers health issues and medical evacuation.

What should I keep in mind when exploring Kenya's beaches?

 
Kenya’s coastal areas offer beautiful beaches for relaxation and water sports. Respect local customs by dressing appropriately and always verify the safety of water activities.

Can anyone climb Mount Kenya?

 
Mount Kenya, Africa’s second-highest peak, is accessible to climbers with various levels of experience. Proper planning, acclimatization, and a guide are essential for a successful and safe climb.

What makes Kenya's wildlife and safari experiences special?

 
Kenya is famed for its diverse wildlife habitats, from the Masai Mara’s rolling grasslands to the Amboseli’s elephant herds against the backdrop of Mount Kilimanjaro. Unique conservancies and national parks offer intimate and sustainable safari experiences.

Which parks in Kenya are best for first-time safari visitors?

 
First-timers are often drawn to the Masai Mara for its abundant wildlife and the chance to witness the wildebeest migration. Amboseli and Lake Nakuru National Parks also offer classic safari experiences with stunning scenery.

What should I consider when traveling to Kenya with kids or elderly people?

 
Kenya offers many family-friendly and accessible safari options. Ensure all vaccinations are up to date, and choose accommodations and activities that cater to the needs of all family members.

How can I celebrate a special occasion in Kenya?

 
Mark your celebration with a sunrise hot air balloon ride over the Masai Mara, a luxury safari in a private conservancy, or a beachside dinner under the stars along the coast.

How does Kenya compare to Tanzania and other safari destinations?

 
Kenya and Tanzania both boast exceptional safari experiences, each with its unique charm. Tanzania offers more secluded safari adventures, renowned for the Great Migration across the Serengeti, providing a sense of untouched wilderness.

On the other hand, Kenya tends to be more accessible and affordable, with Nairobi serving as a vibrant urban base offering a plethora of activities. Its diverse range of restaurants, nightlife, and day trips appeals particularly to budget-conscious and solo travelers seeking a blend of wildlife and city experiences.
